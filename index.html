A Project in Becoming

My first foray into exploring digial media is my "becoming" project. It focuses on who I am, what I like, and my goals for this class and the future.

My second project in exploring digital media is my webtext. It is more experimental.

Branching Webtext

My third and most ambitious project in this phase is my non-linear webtext. It was certainly the most challenging, but also the most interesting. Full of possibillity!

Phase 1 Course Goals


1. Foster the creative habits of mind

In Phase One, the class focused on creating web pages. I consider myself a mildly creative person, however creating web pages was a brand new thing for me. I post and share plenty of my photographs as a photographer, but even that sort of production is still ultimatley a consumption of other websites that host those photos. Learning to be on the other side of that equation; crafting the fabric that the photos and other content exist on is a completely different kind of creativity. Teaching HTML is not easy, and wrapping my brain around that forced me to restructure the way I thought about my conent and how I presented it to the public.

2. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

Workshopping the three web assignments gave me a lot of personal ideas for revision, as well as inspiration for ways to use the digital canvas differently. Personally, I got the most out of small-group workshopping. I beleive small-group workshopping is superior for personal improvement because it's most conducive to multi-way conversations. On a whole-class scale, workshopping can often turn into a presentation. While presentations have their place, they tend to be a one-way event. The audience may ask questions, but there is still little room for growth. Small group revising allowed me to not only better understand what was possible, but get direct suggestions specific to my own vibe and vision.

3. Think deeply about craft.

Designing a branching website is much like writing a story. As a writing major, this point of view was helpful to thinking about user experience when viewing my web-based projects. Craft is about combining mediums for a wholistic, put-together final product.

4. Engage in sustained research and skills development.

In order to complete this project, I had to torubleshoot with both peers and online resources. This helped build my newfound skills doing basic web-coding, along with a healthy dose of trial-and-error.

5. Learn to present your work

Presenting in front of the class, as well as small group workshopping, helped me learn to talk through my work. I find that being able to talk about your work in front of others actually helps me understand my own work better. I know this is true of teaching skills, so it makes sense that it applies to projects like this as well.
